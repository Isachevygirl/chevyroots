The Camaro rental thing went well. So well that now I'm doing it again with a bigger gun.

April 18. I've reserved a 2024 Corvette C8 Stingray from a specialty rental outfit in Northern Virginia. The plan is to drive it north to the entrance of Skyline Drive in Shenandoah National Park, run the entire 105-mile length of the park road, overnight in a cabin near the southern end, and then run it back the next day taking the long way home through the Blue Ridge.

I'm not going to lie. I'm a little scared.

## Why Skyline Drive

If you've never been, Skyline Drive is the road that runs the entire length of Shenandoah National Park along the top of the Blue Ridge. 105 miles, 75 overlooks, and a 35 mph speed limit that I am going to respect.

The speed limit thing is important. Skyline Drive isn't a performance drive. It's a scenic drive. The point isn't to push the car — the point is to be **in** a great car on a great road for five hours while the trees open up into views every quarter mile. The driving rhythm is gentle: long easy sweepers, tight technical switchbacks around the overlooks, elevation changes that let you feel the weight of the car settling and unloading.

In a Civic, Skyline Drive is a nice day out.

In a C8 Corvette, with the V8 rumbling at part throttle through the mountain air, with the top off (I reserved the convertible — more on that in a second), with the overlooks clear and the trees just starting to green up for spring — **I think Skyline Drive in the C8 might be one of the great car experiences I'll ever have.**

## The car — what I'm getting

The specialty rental I'm using has two Corvettes. A 2023 Stingray hardtop in Arctic White with the 1LT trim — the absolute base model. And a 2024 Stingray **convertible** in Torch Red with the 2LT and Z51 performance package.

I reserved the convertible.

I know, I know. "Crystal, the Z06 exists. The E-Ray exists. Why are you renting the base convertible?" Three reasons:

1. **Top-off matters more than horsepower on this drive.** At 35 mph speed limits, I don't need the flat-plane crank of a Z06. I need my hair getting messed up and the Blue Ridge air pouring in. The Stingray convertible's power retractable hardtop does its thing in 16 seconds at up to 30 mph, which is exactly what I want.
2. **The Z51 package is the sweet spot.** Bigger brakes, Magnetic Ride, dry sump oil system, tighter gearing — it's got all the performance hardware that actually matters on a road like this, without jumping another $40K for a Z06.
3. **It was the only one available.** Let's be honest. The Z06 was booked through the summer.

Starting mileage when I pick it up will be around 14,000. I'm getting ~200 miles of included driving for my two-day rental and I'll pay $1.50 a mile over that. My planned route is about 320 miles total, so I'm budgeting for the overage. No point pretending it's a cheap trip.

## The route

**Day 1 — Fredericksburg to Big Meadows (south-ish):**
- I-95 → VA-3 → US-522 → Front Royal (the north entrance to Skyline Drive)
- Run the entire 105-mile Skyline Drive top-to-bottom slowly
- Overnight at a cabin near Big Meadows (booked)

**Day 2 — Big Meadows back to Fredericksburg:**
- Exit Skyline Drive at Rockfish Gap (the south end)
- Pick up US-250 east to Waynesboro
- VA-20 → VA-3 → home
- Planned arrival back at the rental lot by 5 PM

Total driving: ~320 miles over two days. Skyline Drive itself is the middle of day one and takes 4–5 hours with overlook stops. The rest is backroads where I can open it up a little more without feeling bad about fuel economy (it's a C8, the answer to "what's the fuel economy like" is "who cares").

## The questions I'm already worrying about

This is the part where I'm honest about being nervous.

<TipBox label="Things I'm Actually Stressing About">
**Will I scrape the front lip?** The C8's front overhang is low. I've watched videos of owners dragging the nose on driveways, restaurant entrances, gas station dips. The rental has a front lift system that raises the nose 40mm on demand, but I've never used one. I'm going to practice in the parking lot before I leave.

**The weather.** Mid-April in the Blue Ridge is unpredictable. Could be 75 and sunny. Could be 45 and raining sideways. I'm bringing layers and a real raincoat. If it's raining hard I'll still run the drive, but the top will stay on.

**Fuel.** The C8 needs 93 octane. The closest 93-octane stations to Skyline Drive are in Front Royal (north entrance) and Waynesboro (south exit). There's no gas on Skyline Drive itself. I'm filling up before I enter, which means I'm filling up in Front Royal.

**Photography.** I want good photos of the car at the overlooks — this is content I'm going to write about, and I want it to look like a real shoot, not iPhone shots. I'm bringing my real camera. But I don't want to spend the whole drive stopping at every overlook to set up a tripod. I need to pick the 6-8 best overlooks for photos and just shoot those.

**Getting it home clean.** If it rains, the red paint is going to show every water mark. I'm bringing microfiber cloths and quick detailer. The rental company doesn't charge extra for "normal dirt" but I want the car to come back looking like the same car.
</TipBox>

## The thing I keep thinking about

Here's the part I haven't said out loud yet.

I'm writing a Chevy site. I drive a Silverado ZR2 and I love it. Trucks are my lane. But if you really press me on which Chevy I would own if money were no object and I had a second spot in the garage — it's not the ZR2 and it's not a Silverado EV and it's not a Camaro ZL1. It's a Corvette.

I grew up around trucks and the Impalas on Houston streets and the occasional Camaro a friend's dad had. Corvettes were the thing you saw on somebody's lawn at a high-end car show and looked at from a distance. I didn't know anybody who owned one.

<Pullquote>Four days from now I'm going to be driving one through Shenandoah for 48 hours with nothing to do except feel it and pay attention. I genuinely don't know which one is going to happen.</Pullquote>
