Every Chevy website I could find online is one of two things:

Either it's a dealer lead-gen farm — thin articles designed to trick Google, stuffed with affiliate links to extended warranties, funneling readers toward a "get a quote" button that sells them out to a dealership sales manager three hours later.

Or it's an AI-generated spec sheet mill — "Top 10 Chevrolet Silverado Facts You Won't Believe!" — written by nobody in particular, about trucks nobody at the publication has actually driven, for people who deserve better.

There was no third option. There was no Chevy site written by a Chevy person, for Chevy people, that didn't feel like a scam.

So I'm building the third option. This is the first Crystal's Corner.

## Who I Am

I'm Crystal. I grew up in Texas, around Chevys, around the people who work on them and love them. My first vehicle was a used Silverado that taught me more about mechanical patience than any classroom did. My current truck is a Silverado ZR2 — the off-road trim, not a base work truck — and I daily drive it, take it to car shows, and write about it honestly.

I'm not a professional journalist. I'm not a paid-for influencer. I have never accepted a free trip from a dealer or a comped warranty from Endurance. What I am is someone who has been around these trucks and cars my whole life, who has strong opinions about them, and who has gotten tired of watching bad information get written about Chevys by people who don't drive them.

## What ChevyRoots Will Be

The site at launch has about 35 buyer's guides, a database of every Chevy model we cover with known issues by year, regional car show calendars, a mechanic directory, and the start of Crystal's Corner — this column.

Over time, here's what I want it to become:

- **The most honest buying resource** for anyone shopping a Chevy. Guides written by people who actually own the trucks and cars they're reviewing, not by writers who've never sat in one.
- **A real community** for Chevy owners — not just content, but build features, event coverage, a mechanic directory you can actually trust, and eventually a forum-style space where people can compare notes honestly.
- **The site I'd want to read** if I were shopping my first Chevy. The one I couldn't find when I was researching my ZR2.

## What It Won't Be

- **We will never accept payment for favorable coverage.** If I write that a truck is great, it's because I think it's great. If I write that a truck has problems, it's because it has problems.
- **We will never run dealer lead-gen articles disguised as reviews.** Affiliate commissions on things like extended warranty comparisons are disclosed. Articles are never written to funnel you toward the highest-commission product.
- **We will never generate content with AI and pretend it was written by a human.** If I write it, I write it. If the editorial team writes it, they write it. No shortcuts, no faceless content farms.
- **We will never "fact-check" user-submitted build stories into blandness.** If someone writes a paragraph about their grandfather's C10 that's exactly the way they'd tell it in person, that's the paragraph that publishes.

## The Honest Ask

The honest ask in this first column is the same one every independent creator makes: **subscribe to the newsletter**. The newsletter form is at the bottom of every page. One email a week. New guides, new Crystal's Corner, new community builds, and the honest stuff. Unsubscribe whenever.

That's the whole business model for now. A newsletter. A handful of affiliate links on guides that are written to be genuinely useful. Eventually — once there's real traffic — dealer partnerships and OEM press loans that I'll disclose every time.

If this site is going to be what I want it to be, it needs readers who care enough to subscribe. That's you. If you're reading this and you've read this far, you're the reason the site exists.

I'll see you Friday.

— **Crystal**
*Founder, ChevyRoots*
