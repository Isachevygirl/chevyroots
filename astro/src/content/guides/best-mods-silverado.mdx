    <h2 id="leveling-kit">Mod #1 — Leveling Kit</h2>

    <div class="mod-card top-pick">
      <div class="pick-badge">Best Bang for Buck</div>
      <div class="mod-rank">#1 of 8 — Visual + Functional</div>
      <div class="mod-name">Leveling Kit</div>
      <div class="mod-cost">$100–$400 installed</div>
      <div class="mod-specs">
        <div class="spec-pill"><div class="spec-label">Front Lift</div><div class="spec-value good">2–2.5 inches</div></div>
        <div class="spec-pill"><div class="spec-label">Install Time</div><div class="spec-value warn">2–4 hours</div></div>
        <div class="spec-pill"><div class="spec-label">Alignment Needed</div><div class="spec-value good">Yes</div></div>
        <div class="spec-pill"><div class="spec-label">Tire Clearance</div><div class="spec-value good">Up to 33"</div></div>
      </div>
      <p class="mod-desc">The Silverado sits nose-down from the factory — a deliberate design choice to compensate for payload sag. A leveling kit raises the front 2–2.5 inches to match the rear, giving the truck an aggressive, squared-off stance and opening up clearance for larger tires (up to 33" on most setups without rubbing).</p>
      <p class="mod-desc"><strong>Why it tops this list:</strong> At $100–$400 all-in (including alignment), this is the single highest-impact visual and functional mod available. You'll fit bigger tires, get a better stance, and the truck just looks meaner. Most owners do this first — and for good reason.</p>
      <p class="best-for"><strong>Best for:</strong> Anyone who wants a more aggressive stance without a full lift kit</p>

HP gains are real but modest — the biggest benefit is improved throttle response and a better intake sound.</p>

      <p class="mod-desc"><strong>Important:</strong> Tuning will void factory powertrain warranty coverage on related components. Plan accordingly if your truck is still under warranty. Most owners tune after the warranty expires.</p>

    <h2 id="bottom-line">Bottom Line</h2>
    <p>If you're modding your Silverado for the first time, start with the <strong>leveling kit</strong>. It's the highest impact-per-dollar of anything on this list. Follow it with an exhaust if you want the V8 to sound like it should, and an intake if you want crisper throttle response.</p>
    <p>If your truck is out of warranty, a tune on top of those three mods turns the 5.3L into a noticeably different truck. That combo — level, exhaust, intake, tune — is the Silverado enthusiast's starter kit for a reason.</p>
