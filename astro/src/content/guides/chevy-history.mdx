            <h2>Two Men, One <span class="gold">Dream</span></h2>
            <p>
              On November 3, 1911, in Detroit, Michigan, race car driver <strong>Louis Chevrolet</strong> and ousted General Motors founder <strong>William C. "Billy" Durant</strong> incorporated the Chevrolet Motor Car Company. Louis brought the racing pedigree and engineering genius; Durant brought the business savvy and burning ambition to reclaim his automotive empire.
            </p>
            <p>
              Their first car &mdash; the <strong>Classic Six</strong> &mdash; debuted in 1912 as a luxury touring car priced at $2,150 (about $68,000 today). But it was Durant's pivot to affordable, reliable cars for everyday Americans that would define Chevrolet's soul. By 1917, Durant had used Chevrolet stock to regain control of General Motors, and the Chevy brand was folded into the GM family.
            </p>
            <p>
              Louis Chevrolet, frustrated by Durant's shift away from performance, left the company that bore his name. But his legacy &mdash; speed, innovation, and the iconic bowtie &mdash; would endure for over a century.
            </p>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1910">
            <div class="timeline-year">1911</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevrolet Motor Car Company Founded</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Louis Chevrolet and William C. Durant incorporate the company in Detroit, Michigan on November 3rd.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Founding</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1910">
            <div class="timeline-year">1912</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">The Classic Six Debuts</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy's first production car &mdash; a luxury touring car with a 299 cubic-inch six-cylinder engine producing 40 horsepower. Price: $2,150.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">First Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1910">
            <div class="timeline-year">1913</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">The Bowtie Is Born</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The iconic Chevrolet bowtie logo first appears. Its origin remains debated &mdash; Durant claimed he was inspired by a wallpaper pattern in a Paris hotel; his wife said it came from a newspaper ad.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Branding</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1910">
            <div class="timeline-year">1914</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Baby Grand &amp; Royal Mail</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet introduces the affordable Baby Grand touring car and Royal Mail roadster, priced under $1,000. Durant's strategy to compete with Ford's Model T begins to take shape.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Model Launch</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1910">
            <div class="timeline-year">1917</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevrolet Joins General Motors</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Durant uses Chevrolet stock to regain control of GM. Chevrolet becomes a division of General Motors, beginning a partnership that would last over a century.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Milestone</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1920">
            <div class="timeline-year">1923</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Copper-Cooled Engine Experiment</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet briefly experiments with an air-cooled "copper-cooled" engine. The project fails commercially but demonstrates Chevy's willingness to push engineering boundaries early on.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Innovation</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1920">
            <div class="timeline-year">1927</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevy Outsells Ford for the First Time</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Capitol and National series help Chevrolet surpass Ford in annual sales for the first time, producing over one million vehicles. The rivalry that would define American auto history begins.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Sales Milestone</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1920">
            <div class="timeline-year">1929</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">The "Stovebolt Six" Arrives</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet introduces its overhead-valve inline-six engine, nicknamed the "Stovebolt" for its slotted-head bolts. Affordable, reliable, and smooth &mdash; it kept Chevy competitive through the Depression.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Innovation</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1930">
            <div class="timeline-year">1935</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevrolet Suburban Introduced</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Suburban Carryall debuts as one of the first production all-steel station wagons &mdash; and the longest-running nameplate in automotive history, still in production 90+ years later.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1930">
            <div class="timeline-year">1937</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevy Becomes America's #1</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet solidifies its position as the best-selling car brand in America with refined styling, knee-action independent front suspension, and prices everyday families could afford.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Sales Milestone</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1940">
            <div class="timeline-year">1942</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Civilian Production Halted for WWII</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet ceases all civilian car production and converts its factories to build military trucks, aircraft engines, shells, and gun parts. Over the next three years, Chevy plants produce more war materiel than any other GM division.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">WWII</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1940">
            <div class="timeline-year">1945</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Post-War Production Resumes</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">With the war over, Chevrolet restarts civilian production. Pent-up demand creates massive waitlists. The '46 Fleetmaster and Stylemaster sedans fly off lots as America returns to peacetime.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Milestone</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1940">
            <div class="timeline-year">1948</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Advance Design Trucks</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy introduces the Advance Design truck line &mdash; the first major post-war truck redesign. With rounded fenders and improved cabs, they become the best-selling trucks in America and remain popular restoration builds today.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1950">
            <div class="timeline-year">1950</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Bel Air Nameplate Debuts</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Bel Air arrives as a premium hardtop, soon to become the quintessential symbol of 1950s American car culture. The '55, '56, and '57 Tri-Five models are now among the most collected classics ever.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1950">
            <div class="timeline-year">1953</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Corvette &mdash; America's Sports Car Is Born</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The first Corvette rolls off the line in Flint, Michigan &mdash; a two-seat roadster with a fiberglass body and Blue Flame six-cylinder engine. Only 300 are built in the first year, all in Polo White.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Legendary Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1950">
            <div class="timeline-year">1955</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">The Small-Block V8 Revolution</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy introduces the 265 cubic-inch small-block V8 &mdash; arguably the most important engine in automotive history. Lightweight, powerful, and endlessly modifiable, it transformed Chevy's image overnight from "stodgy" to performance leader and became the most produced engine architecture in history.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Innovation</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1950">
            <div class="timeline-year">1957</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">The '57 Chevy &mdash; An Icon Is Made</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The 1957 Bel Air becomes one of the most recognizable cars ever built. With fuel injection available for the first time on a production Chevy, the '57 is fast, beautiful, and destined for eternal cool.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1950">
            <div class="timeline-year">1958</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Impala Takes the Stage</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Impala debuts as a top-of-the-line Bel Air trim, becoming its own model in 1959. It would become the best-selling car in America and a pillar of lowrider culture for generations.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1959</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">El Camino &mdash; Car or Truck?</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy answers Ford's Ranchero with the El Camino, a car-truck hybrid built on the full-size platform. Discontinued in 1960, it returned in 1964 on the Chevelle platform and became a cult classic.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1963</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Corvette Sting Ray Split-Window</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The C2 Corvette Sting Ray arrives with independent rear suspension and the legendary split-window coupe design. Produced for one year only, the '63 split-window is now one of the most valuable Corvettes ever made.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Legendary Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1964</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevelle &amp; the Muscle Car Era</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Chevelle SS launches as Chevy's entry into the muscle car wars. The 1970 Chevelle SS 454 with its LS6 engine (450 hp) is considered one of the greatest muscle cars ever built.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Muscle Era</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1966</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">See the USA in Your Chevrolet</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Dinah Shore's iconic TV jingle becomes one of the most recognized advertising slogans in American history, cementing Chevrolet's place in pop culture and the American road trip dream.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Cultural</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1967</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Camaro &mdash; Ford Has a Problem</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevrolet's answer to the Ford Mustang arrives: the Camaro. With options from inline-six economy to the legendary Z/28 and SS 396, it instantly becomes a pony car icon. First-gen Camaros are among the most valuable muscle cars today.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Legendary Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1960">
            <div class="timeline-year">1969</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">COPO Camaro &mdash; The Underground Legend</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy dealers use the Central Office Production Order system to install the 427 V8 in Camaros &mdash; creating the COPO 9560, one of the rarest and most sought-after muscle cars in history. Only 1,015 were built.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Rare &amp; Collectible</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1970">
            <div class="timeline-year">1970</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Monte Carlo &amp; the Personal Luxury Era</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Monte Carlo arrives as Chevy's personal luxury coupe. Through six generations (1970-2007), it becomes a NASCAR icon and a staple of American driveways.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1970">
            <div class="timeline-year">1973</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">C/K Trucks Dominate</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy's "Square Body" C/K trucks (1973-1987) begin production. Rugged, reliable, and now highly collectible, they defined the American truck for a generation and remain some of the most popular restoration projects.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1970">
            <div class="timeline-year">1976</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevette &mdash; Chevy Goes Small</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">In the wake of the oil crisis, Chevrolet introduces the Chevette &mdash; a small, fuel-efficient hatchback. It sells over 2.8 million units and proves Chevy can adapt to changing times.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Model Launch</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1980">
            <div class="timeline-year">1982</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Third-Gen Camaro Arrives</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The sleek, aerodynamic third-generation Camaro launches with a dramatic wedge shape. The Z28 returns as the performance flagship, and the car is selected as the Indianapolis 500 pace car in its debut year.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Iconic Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1980">
            <div class="timeline-year">1985</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">IROC-Z &mdash; Peak '80s Muscle</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Camaro IROC-Z debuts with tuned suspension, 305 V8 power, and aggressive styling that defines a decade. Named after the International Race of Champions series, the IROC-Z becomes the muscle car of the 1980s.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Muscle Era</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1980">
            <div class="timeline-year">1983</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Monte Carlo SS Returns</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">After a brief hiatus, the Monte Carlo SS comes back with a 180-hp 305 V8 and aero body kit. It dominates NASCAR with 23 wins in its first season and becomes a street icon with its black-and-silver styling.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Muscle Era</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1980">
            <div class="timeline-year">1988</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">C/K 1500 &mdash; The GMT400 Era</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy launches the GMT400 full-size truck platform with improved aerodynamics, fuel injection, and a more refined interior. It modernizes the American pickup and sets the stage for the Silverado nameplate.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1990">
            <div class="timeline-year">1990</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">454 SS &mdash; The Hot Rod Pickup</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy drops the 454 big-block V8 into the C1500 short-bed, creating the 454 SS &mdash; a factory hot rod truck. Low production numbers and raw muscle make it a collector favorite today.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1990">
            <div class="timeline-year">1997</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">C5 Corvette &mdash; World-Class at Last</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The C5 Corvette arrives with an all-new hydroformed frame, rear-mounted transaxle, and the LS1 V8. It delivers genuine supercar handling at an American price and launches the LS engine dynasty.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Legendary Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="1990">
            <div class="timeline-year">1999</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Silverado Nameplate Launches</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Silverado name &mdash; previously a trim level &mdash; becomes its own model. It quickly rises to become one of the best-selling vehicles in America, rivaling the Ford F-150 for truck supremacy.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2006</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Corvette Z06 &mdash; Track Weapon</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The C6 Z06 arrives with a 505-hp LS7 7.0L V8, aluminum frame, and carbon fiber fenders. It demolishes European exotics costing three times as much and proves the Corvette belongs on the world stage.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Performance</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2010</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevrolet Volt &mdash; Electric Pioneer</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The Volt plug-in hybrid launches as one of the first mass-market electric vehicles from a major automaker, signaling Chevy's commitment to electrification years before the EV boom.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Innovation</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2014</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Colorado Returns</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">After a four-year absence, the Chevy Colorado returns as a right-sized midsize truck. It wins Motor Trend Truck of the Year and proves there's still a massive market for trucks that aren't full-size.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Trucks</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2017</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">Chevrolet Bolt EV</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">The all-electric Bolt EV arrives with 238 miles of range at under $37,500 &mdash; making affordable long-range EVs a reality for everyday drivers, years before many competitors.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Electric</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2020</div>
            <div class="timeline-title">C8 Corvette &mdash; Mid-Engine Revolution</div>
            <div class="timeline-desc">After nearly 70 years, the Corvette goes mid-engine. The C8 Stingray delivers supercar performance starting under $60K, shattering the price-to-performance barrier and earning universal acclaim.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Legendary Model</span>
          </div>

          <div class="timeline-item" data-decade="2000">
            <div class="timeline-year">2024</div>

            <div class="timeline-desc">Chevy goes all-in on electric with the Silverado EV (up to 400+ miles of range) and the affordable Equinox EV starting around $33,000. The Ultium platform powers a new generation.</div>
            <span class="timeline-tag">Electric Future</span>
          </div>
