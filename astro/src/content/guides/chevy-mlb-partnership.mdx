<h2 id="partnership">The Partnership</h2>

      <p>Chevrolet became the <strong>Official Vehicle of Major League Baseball in 2005</strong> — and twenty-plus years later, it's still one of the most visible and enduring brand relationships in American sports. Think about what has changed in that time: stadiums have come and gone, dynasties have been built and broken, streaming has completely disrupted how we watch the game. But every fall, when the confetti flies and the MVP walks to the podium, there's a Chevy on the field.</p>

      <p>The partnership operates at every level of the sport. <strong>Chevy branding appears on broadcast graphics</strong> — those lower-third overlays during national TV broadcasts — as well as in-stadium signage at all 30 ballparks, across MLB's digital platforms, and on official league merchandise and communications. When you're watching a game on Fox or ESPN and you see a branded segment or a sponsored replay, there's a real chance the Chevy bowtie is in that frame.</p>

      <p>But the crown jewel of the whole deal — the thing that makes it iconic — is the <strong>World Series MVP Award</strong>. More on that in a minute.</p>

      <div class="highlight-box">
        <div class="highlight-box-title">Why This Partnership Works</div>
        <p>Baseball and Chevrolet share the same DNA: quintessentially American, deeply regional, built on loyalty that passes down through generations. A kid who grows up going to Astros games with their dad doesn't just become an Astros fan — they pick up all of it. The food, the rituals, the truck in the parking lot. Chevy understood this alignment long before most brands figured out sports sponsorship.</p>
      </div>

      <p>This is widely regarded as one of the <strong>largest sports sponsorships in the automotive industry</strong>. MLB's reach — over 70 million attendees per season pre-pandemic, plus hundreds of millions of broadcast viewers — gives Chevy consistent, long-form exposure across a demographic that actually buys trucks and SUVs. It's not just logo placement. It's cultural integration.</p>

      <h2 id="mvp">The World Series MVP Award</h2>

      <p>Every year, after the final out of the World Series, one player is named MVP of the Fall Classic. And every year, on the field in the aftermath of a championship, <strong>Chevrolet hands that player the keys to a brand new vehicle</strong>. Usually a Silverado. Sometimes something even more special.</p>

      <div class="inline-img">
        <div class="img-label">

      <p>The presentation happens right there in the stadium, with the championship crowd still going, confetti still in the air. It's one of those moments that has become part of the World Series ritual — you watch for the trophy, the champagne, and the Chevy. In order.</p>

      <h2 id="stadiums">Chevy at Every Stadium</h2>

      <p>One of the ongoing projects at ChevyRoots is documenting the Chevy presence at all 30 MLB ballparks. Every stadium in the league carries Chevy branding in some form — signage, digital boards, sponsored sections, concourse installations, local dealer partnerships. The goal is to build a field guide: what to look for, where to find it, and what the Chevy experience looks like market by market.</p>

      <h3>What to Look For</h3>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Chevy signage locations</strong> — outfield walls, foul poles, video board lower thirds during play</li>
        <li><strong>Chevy-sponsored sections</strong> — some parks have branded home run areas, bleacher sections, or club spaces</li>
        <li><strong>Local dealer tie-ins</strong> — regional dealers often run activations, giveaways, and tailgate partnerships</li>
        <li><strong>Broadcast overlays</strong> — when the camera cuts to a sponsored replay or stat, check the logo</li>
        <li><strong>Parking lot activations</strong> — pregame events, test drive opportunities, tailgate setups near main gates</li>
      </ul>

      <p>We're visiting stadiums and documenting the Chevy presence. Follow along as this guide fills out throughout the 2026 season — every visit gets logged with photos, notes on placement, and the full tailgate situation.</p>
