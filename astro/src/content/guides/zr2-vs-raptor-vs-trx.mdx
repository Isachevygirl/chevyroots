I got a day with all three factory off-road flagship half-tons. My own ZR2. A 2024 Ford F-150 Raptor (37-inch package). And a 2023 Ram 1500 TRX, courtesy of a friend of a friend at a Ram dealer who let me drive his dealer-plated demo for two hours on-road and one hour off.

This is not a review written from press loans or photo shoots. It's what I noticed driving all three on the same day, back to back, on the same roads and the same trail sections. Here's the honest take.

## The Trucks

**Silverado ZR2** — my truck. 6.2 V8, 420 hp, 460 lb-ft, Multimatic DSSV dampers, 33" Goodyear Wrangler Territory MTs, front and rear lockers, 10-speed automatic. MSRP in the mid-$70s.

**Ford F-150 Raptor (37 package)** — 3.5 EcoBoost twin-turbo V6, 450 hp, 510 lb-ft, FOX Live Valve dampers, 37" tires, rear locker only, 10-speed automatic. MSRP around $85K.

**Ram 1500 TRX** — 6.2 supercharged Hellcat V8, 702 hp, 650 lb-ft, Bilstein Black Hawk e² dampers, 35" Goodyear Wrangler Territory tires, front and rear lockers, 8-speed automatic. MSRP around $100K (discontinued in 2024, but still on dealer lots).

All three are built to do different things even though they're marketed as competitors.

## On-Road Impressions

### Silverado ZR2
Plants itself. The DSSV dampers eat expansion joints and lane changes without drama. Steering is direct for a truck. The 6.2 pulls linearly from 1,500 rpm and just keeps going. Ride quality is honestly better than some SUVs I've driven. You can daily this truck and not hate the experience.

### Ford F-150 Raptor (37s)
Feels taller than the ZR2 because it IS taller. The 37-inch tire package gives you approach angles that are borderline ridiculous but on-road you're sitting higher and the truck feels a little more vague. The FOX Live Valves adapt on the fly and the truck handles highway sweepers well, but at lower speeds on rough pavement there's a noticeable float the ZR2 doesn't have. The 3.5 EcoBoost is torquey and smooth, turbos barely noticeable, but the exhaust note is... muted. Like a luxury SUV with a slight growl. Not bad, just not what I wanted from a truck that looks like the Raptor does.

### Ram 1500 TRX
The TRX is a different animal. The moment you start it, it sounds like a muscle car at idle. Give it any throttle and the supercharger whine announces itself. The Bilsteins are tuned softer than the Multimatics or the FOX Live Valves, so you get more body roll in corners and more float on highway dips — but the trade-off is a Cadillac-smooth ride on rough pavement. It's a truck that makes you feel like you're driving something extraordinary every time you turn the key.

<Pullquote>The TRX is the most theatrical of the three. The ZR2 is the most composed. The Raptor is somewhere in the middle, leaning toward the TRX.</Pullquote>

**My pick for on-road, daily driving comfort: ZR2.** The Multimatics are the best damper setup on pavement of the three. It's not close.

## Acceleration and Sound

This one matters to people, so let's be honest:

- **TRX** feels the fastest. The 702 hp supercharged Hellcat moves the 6,900-pound truck in a way that is genuinely shocking. 0–60 in the mid-4s. The sound is unapologetic and present in the cabin at all times.
- **Raptor 37** is faster than you expect for a 3.5 V6. Low 5s to 60. The sound is underwhelming — EcoBoost V6s don't make music.
- **ZR2** is the "slowest" on paper (mid-5s to 60) but the 6.2 sounds the best of the three V8s in my opinion, and the power delivery is the most linear. The TRX dumps 702 hp on you; the ZR2 rolls it out smoothly. Different experiences.

**My pick for acceleration: TRX (objectively). My pick for engine character: ZR2 (subjective — I love the 6.2).**

## Interior & Ergonomics

### Silverado ZR2
Significantly improved from pre-2022 Silverados. 13.4" touchscreen, proper heads-up display, clean layout, decent materials. Still not a Ram 1500 Limited for luxury, but it's no longer the clear loser of the big three. The seats are supportive and the ZR2-specific trim pieces are nice. Nothing embarrassing.

### Ford F-150 Raptor
Ford went hard on the Raptor interior. Giant center screen, blue accents, Recaro-ish seats that hold you in place. Best steering wheel of the three — the chunky flat-bottom design feels right. Panel gaps are inconsistent in spots (Ford truck tradition) but overall the cabin is a nice place to be.

### Ram 1500 TRX
Best interior of the three, no contest. Ram has been dominating truck interiors for years and the TRX is no exception. The materials are luxury-tier, the screen is big and smart, the seats are the best. If interior quality is your deciding factor, Ram wins.

<TipBox label="Interior Ranking">
Ram TRX > Ford Raptor > Silverado ZR2. All three are acceptable; the TRX is noticeably above. If you're spending $75-100K on a truck and you sit in it for hours a week, this matters.
</TipBox>

## Off-Road

This is where the trucks start to really differentiate.

### Silverado ZR2
The ZR2's strength is the combination of the Multimatic DSSVs and the dual lockers. The DSSV dampers soak up high-speed impacts without bouncing back. Once you engage both lockers (press the button, put it in low), the ZR2 just walks over stuff. The factory approach/departure angles are good; the 33s are adequate. It's not the most capable of the three on paper, but it's the most composed — the truck doesn't get excited on technical terrain. It just handles it.

### Ford F-150 Raptor (37 Package)
The 37s and the longer wheel travel give the Raptor an off-road envelope the other two can't match. It WILL bash through a washed-out section of trail at 45 mph and make you feel like a professional. The FOX Live Valves adapt on the fly and the truck at speed in the desert is a genuine weapon. BUT — and this is important — the rear-only locker is a real limitation on technical crawling. On rocks, where I want both diffs locked, the Raptor is at a disadvantage.

### Ram 1500 TRX
The TRX is fast off-road, not technical off-road. The Bilsteins are tuned for high-speed desert running, similar to the Raptor. But the TRX weighs 6,900 pounds — nearly a thousand more than the Raptor or ZR2 — and that mass shows up in soft sand and mud. It will GO, because 702 hp solves most problems, but it gets stuck in situations the ZR2 walks through. And the fuel economy off-road is horrendous.

**My pick for technical off-road: ZR2.** (Front locker + Multimatics + composed chassis.)
**My pick for high-speed desert: Raptor.** (37s + longer travel.)
**My pick for straight-line brute force: TRX.** (702 hp.)

## Fuel Economy

Don't buy any of these for fuel economy, but:

- **ZR2 (6.2)**: 15.8 mpg combined real-world (I'm the owner; this is my actual average)
- **Raptor (3.5 EB)**: 15–16 combined from reviews I trust
- **TRX**: 12 combined on a good day, 10 if you have any fun with it

If fuel cost is a decision criterion, the TRX is the worst by a wide margin. The ZR2 and Raptor are a wash in practice.

## Price and Value

- **ZR2**: ~$76K out the door
- **Raptor (37 pkg)**: ~$85K out the door
- **TRX**: ~$100K out the door (and discontinued, so dealer inventory only)

For the $24K premium over the ZR2, the TRX gives you: way more horsepower, a Hellcat sound, and a better interior. It does NOT give you better composure on pavement, better technical off-road, or better fuel economy.

For the $9K premium over the ZR2, the Raptor gives you: 37-inch factory tires, longer wheel travel, better high-speed desert ability, and a bigger screen. It does NOT give you: front locker capability, better on-pavement manners, or a V8.

<CrystalTake>
If someone handed me a blank check and told me to pick one of these three trucks:

- **For the best blend of on-road composure, technical off-road capability, and V8 character: ZR2.** (This is why I own it.)
- **For maximum high-speed desert shenanigans: Raptor with the 37 package.**
- **For absolute 702-hp tire-shredding theater and the best interior: TRX.**

But with my own money? The ZR2 is still the right answer for me. It costs $24K less than the TRX, it daily-drives better than the Raptor, it's objectively more composed on pavement than either, and the front locker capability matters for the kind of off-roading I actually do. The TRX is louder and more dramatic. The Raptor is more capable at speed. The ZR2 is more *right* for how I actually use a truck.
</CrystalTake>

## The Category Winners

- **Best on-road daily driver**: Silverado ZR2
- **Best highway sound and feel**: Ram TRX
- **Best acceleration**: Ram TRX
- **Best high-speed desert running**: Ford F-150 Raptor (37 pkg)
- **Best technical / crawling off-road**: Silverado ZR2 (dual lockers)
- **Best interior**: Ram TRX
- **Best value**: Silverado ZR2
- **Most fuel-efficient**: Tied between ZR2 and Raptor (both around 15-16 real mpg)
- **Worst fuel economy**: Ram TRX (10-12 mpg)

## Which Should YOU Buy?

**Buy the ZR2 if:**
- You want the best on-road manners of the three
- You do technical off-roading (rocks, crawling, tight trails)
- You want the best value
- The V8 character and sound matter to you
- You're going to daily-drive it and need fuel economy to be "OK" not "terrible"

**Buy the Raptor (37) if:**
- You actually run desert or BLM at speed
- You want the most factory capability at speed
- You prefer Ford's interior and tech to Chevy's
- You don't need the V8 sound (you'll get turbo V6 instead)

**Buy the TRX if:**
- Straight-line power matters more than everything else
- The Hellcat sound is the whole point
- You have money to burn on fuel
- You want the best interior
- You're OK with a less composed ride and dramatically worse fuel economy

**Don't buy any of these if:**
- You're never actually going to use the off-road capability
- A base Silverado LT with a 6.2 would do everything you need for $25K less

## The Biggest Surprise From the Day

The ZR2 held its own against trucks that cost $10-25K more. I expected to drive the Raptor and the TRX and feel envy. I didn't. I felt validated. The Multimatic suspension setup is genuinely category-leading on pavement, and the dual lockers matter more on the trails I run than extra horsepower does.

The TRX was the most *fun* for 10 minutes. The Raptor was the most *capable* at speed. The ZR2 was the most *right* for the life I actually live.
