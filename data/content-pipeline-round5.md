- Verdict: Tahoe is a competent camper tower for moderate setups; know your limits before you buy

**Monetization:** Weight distribution hitch affiliate links, sway control products, comparison funnel into Suburban content (see Category B).

### 9. Equinox EV Real-World Range in Winter: What Owners Are Actually Getting vs. What GM Says

**Why this exists:** This is the hottest topic on equinoxevforum.com. Multiple threads document range dropping to 102 miles in extreme cold (10–25°F) — one-third of the advertised range. InsideEVs published a full video. This content converts because $35K+ buyers need the honest truth.

**Suggested title:** "Chevy Equinox EV Winter Range: Owners Are Getting 102 Miles When GM Says 319. Here's What You Need to Know"

**Target audience:** Prospective Equinox EV buyers in cold-weather states (Northeast, Midwest, Mountain West), current owners trying to understand and manage range anxiety.

**Key talking points:**
- Advertised range: up to 319 miles (FWD) / 280 miles (AWD)
- Real-world winter range: 30–40% reduction is common; 60–67% reduction reported at 10–25°F
- AWD vs FWD in winter: AWD improves traction but costs range — the tradeoff most buyers don't understand
- Heat pump: the Equinox EV has one; it helps up to 10% vs resistive heat, but not enough to prevent significant cold-weather loss
- Preconditioning: the single best tool for managing winter range — how to use it effectively
- Practical planning tips: charging infrastructure for cold-weather EV ownership
- Who this matters for and who it doesn't: city commuters under 50 miles/day are largely unaffected; road-trippers in Minnesota are not

**Monetization:** EV charging affiliate links, home charger installation partnerships, cold-weather accessory links.
