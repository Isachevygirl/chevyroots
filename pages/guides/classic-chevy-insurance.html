Classic Chevy Insurance: Hagerty vs Grundy vs American Collectors

By Crystal & ChevyRoots Staff
Updated April 2026
7 min read
~1,000 words

Why Classic Cars Need Special Insurance

Your standard auto insurance policy is designed for daily-driven vehicles that depreciate over time. Classic cars are the opposite — they appreciate. A '57 Bel Air in good condition is worth more today than it was five years ago, and far more than it would be under your regular insurer's "actual cash value" calculation, which factors in age and depreciation.

If a standard insurer totals your classic, they pay current market value minus depreciation. For a car from 1957, that calculation is a disaster. Classic car insurance solves this with agreed value coverage — you and the insurer agree on the car's value upfront, and that's what you get paid if it's totaled or stolen. No depreciation. No arguments. No surprises.

Agreed Value vs. Stated Value vs. Actual Cash Value

Agreed Value: You and the insurer agree on a specific value. That exact amount is paid at total loss. Best option for classics.
Stated Value: You state a value, but the insurer can pay up to that amount — not necessarily the full amount. Less protective than agreed value.
Actual Cash Value: Market value minus depreciation. Terrible for classics. Never accept this for a collector car.

Hagerty — The Category Standard

Hagerty
The largest classic car insurer in North America
Most Popular

Hagerty is the dominant name in collector car insurance for a reason. They built their business on enthusiast culture — not just insurance. Hagerty membership includes access to their valuation database (the most cited source for classic car prices), a roadside assistance program specifically for classics, and a community platform that connects collectors.

Their insurance product offers agreed value coverage (not stated value) on all collector vehicles. Mileage is flexible — they offer several tiers from "occasional use" (under 1,000 miles/year) to "limited use" (up to 6,000 miles), and can accommodate higher mileage with underwriting approval. Coverage requires the vehicle to be garaged and not used as a daily driver.

Hagerty is particularly strong for trucks — they've explicitly positioned themselves in the classic truck and restomod space. If your '69 Camaro is also your weekend driver that goes to 4 shows a year, Hagerty has a program for that.

Strengths
  • True agreed value — no depreciation disputes
  • Flexible mileage options
  • Best-in-class valuation database
  • Spartan roadside for classics
  • Strong for restomod and modified classics
  • Covers driving to and from shows
Limitations
  • Garaging requirement is strict
  • Not the cheapest option
  • Daily-driven classics are restricted
  • Some state availability limitations

Grundy — Best Value for Show Cars

Grundy
Since 1947 — the original specialty insurer
Best Value

Grundy has been insuring collector cars since 1947 — longer than any other specialty insurer. They pioneered the agreed value concept for classic cars and remain a top choice, especially for pure show cars with very limited use.

Their key differentiator is pricing. Grundy tends to come in significantly lower than Hagerty on premium, particularly for cars driven fewer than 1,000 miles per year. They also offer no mileage restrictions on some policies — you get a policy limit instead of a mileage cap, which works well for cars that occasionally go to events.

The tradeoff: their digital experience and online tools lag behind Hagerty. Getting a quote requires more manual steps, and their valuation process is less automated. For experienced collectors who know their car's value and just want solid, affordable coverage, Grundy is outstanding. For newer collectors who want the full ecosystem, Hagerty may serve better.

Strengths
  • Often the lowest premium
  • True agreed value since inception
  • No mileage cap on some policies
  • Excellent for low-use show cars
  • Long track record, strong reputation
Limitations
  • Dated digital experience
  • Less flexible for higher-mileage use
  • Valuation process less automated
  • Limited membership perks vs. Hagerty

American Collectors — Best for Daily-Driven Classics

American Collectors
Flexible coverage for drivers, not just show pieces
Most Flexible

American Collectors Insurance stands out for one key reason: they'll insure classics that are driven regularly, including daily drivers and those without garaging requirements in some programs. If your daily is a '69 Camaro or you drive your Chevelle to work on Fridays, this is your company.

They offer agreed value coverage and competitive rates, with programs specifically designed for weekend drivers and even some daily-driven applications. Their "Pleasure Use" policy covers regular use beyond the show-only restrictions you'd find elsewhere. They also offer coverage for unfinished projects — useful if you're mid-restoration on a Bel Air and your garage catches fire before the car is complete.

Premium tends to fall between Grundy and Hagerty depending on use pattern. For high-mileage classic drivers, American Collectors is often significantly cheaper than the alternatives because they've priced regular use into their product.

Strengths
  • Best for regularly driven classics
  • No strict garaging requirements
  • Covers unfinished restorations
  • Agreed value on all policies
  • Flexible use classifications
Limitations
  • Less brand recognition/ecosystem
  • Higher premium for low-use cars vs. Grundy
  • Smaller community/events presence

Questions to Ask Any Insurer

  • Is this agreed value or stated value? — Only accept agreed value for a classic
  • What are the mileage restrictions? — Know the exact limit before you sign
  • Is garaging required? — And what qualifies as "garaging"?
  • How are modifications handled? — Restomods, engine swaps, and upgrades need to be disclosed and covered
  • What's the claims process? — Ask specifically about total loss process and how value disputes are resolved
  • Can I use my regular mechanic? — Some classic insurers require shop approval on claims
